Attending

Venue

The Web Conference 2026 will be held in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. Dubai is a global hub for business, tourism, and innovation, making it an ideal location for The Web Conference.

The specific venue details will be announced closer to the conference date. Dubai offers world-class conference facilities with state-of-the-art technology and amenities.

Travel Information

Getting to Dubai

Dubai is easily accessible from around the world, with direct flights to over 240 destinations through Dubai International Airport (DXB), one of the world's busiest international airports.

Visa Information

Visitors to the United Arab Emirates may require a visa depending on their nationality. Many countries are eligible for visa on arrival or visa-free entry. Please check the official UAE government website or contact your nearest UAE embassy or consulate for the most up-to-date visa requirements.

The conference organizers will provide invitation letters for visa purposes to registered participants upon request.

Local Transportation

Dubai has an excellent public transportation system, including the Dubai Metro, buses, and taxis. The Dubai Metro is a convenient way to travel around the city, with stations connecting major areas and attractions.

Taxis are readily available and can be hailed on the street or booked through ride-hailing apps. Uber and Careem (a local ride-hailing service) operate in Dubai.

Accommodation

Dubai offers a wide range of accommodation options to suit all budgets, from luxury hotels to more affordable options. The conference organizers will negotiate special rates with selected hotels near the conference venue.

A list of recommended hotels and booking information will be provided closer to the conference date.

Things to Do in Dubai

Dubai offers a wide range of attractions and activities for visitors. Here are some highlights:

Attractions

  • Burj Khalifa: The world's tallest building, offering spectacular views from its observation decks.
  • Dubai Mall: One of the world's largest shopping malls, featuring over 1,200 shops, an aquarium, and an indoor ice rink.
  • Palm Jumeirah: An artificial archipelago in the shape of a palm tree, home to luxury hotels, restaurants, and beaches.
  • Dubai Marina: A waterfront promenade with restaurants, shops, and yacht cruises.
  • Dubai Museum: Located in the historic Al Fahidi Fort, showcasing the history and culture of Dubai.
  • Dubai Creek: A natural seawater inlet that divides the city into two parts, offering traditional abra (water taxi) rides.

Activities

  • Desert Safari: Experience the desert landscape with dune bashing, camel riding, and traditional entertainment.
  • Water Parks: Enjoy Aquaventure Waterpark at Atlantis, The Palm or Wild Wadi Waterpark.
  • Shopping: Explore traditional souks (markets) for gold, spices, and textiles, or visit modern shopping malls.
  • Dining: Dubai offers a diverse culinary scene with restaurants serving cuisines from around the world.
  • Beach Activities: Relax on Dubai's beautiful beaches or try water sports like jet skiing and parasailing.
