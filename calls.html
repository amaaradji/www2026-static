Calls for Submissions

Research Tracks

The Web Conference 2026 invites submissions of original research papers on all topics related to the World Wide Web. The conference will feature multiple research tracks, each focusing on a specific area of Web research.

Important Dates

  • Abstract Registration: October 1, 2025
  • Paper Submission: October 8, 2025
  • Notification: January 10, 2026
  • Camera-Ready: February 10, 2026

Research Tracks

Web Search and Mining

This track focuses on research related to search engines, information retrieval, data mining, and knowledge discovery on the Web.

Web and Society

This track explores the social aspects of the Web, including social networks, social media, social computing, and the impact of the Web on society.

Web Infrastructure

This track covers research on the underlying infrastructure of the Web, including protocols, standards, and technologies that enable the Web to function.

Web of Data

This track focuses on research related to the Semantic Web, linked data, knowledge graphs, and other data-centric aspects of the Web.

Web Security and Privacy

This track covers research on security and privacy issues related to the Web, including authentication, authorization, encryption, and privacy-preserving technologies.

Web Economics, Monetization, and Online Markets

This track explores economic aspects of the Web, including online advertising, e-commerce, cryptocurrencies, and business models for the Web.

User Modeling and Personalization

This track focuses on research related to user modeling, personalization, recommendation systems, and adaptive Web systems.

Web Content Analysis, Semantics, and Knowledge

This track covers research on content analysis, natural language processing, knowledge extraction, and semantic understanding of Web content.

Submission Guidelines

All submissions must be original and not simultaneously submitted to another journal or conference. Papers must be submitted in PDF format according to the ACM template.

Research track papers can be either full papers (up to 10 pages) or short papers (up to 6 pages), plus unlimited pages for references and appendices.

All submissions will be peer-reviewed by at least three members of the program committee. Accepted papers will be presented at the conference and published in the conference proceedings.

For detailed submission instructions, please visit the conference submission system (available in September 2025).

Workshops

The Web Conference 2026 invites proposals for workshops to be held in conjunction with the main conference. Workshops provide an opportunity to discuss and explore emerging topics in Web research and development.

Important Dates

  • Workshop Proposal Submission: September 1, 2025
  • Notification: October 15, 2025
  • Workshop Papers Due: January 20, 2026

Submission Guidelines

Workshop proposals should include the following information:

  • Workshop title and acronym
  • Names and affiliations of organizers
  • Workshop description and topics of interest
  • Expected number of participants
  • Planned format and activities
  • Proposed duration (half-day or full-day)
  • Previous editions of the workshop (if any)

Workshop proposals should be submitted via email to workshops@thewebconf2026.org.

Tutorials

The Web Conference 2026 invites proposals for tutorials to be held in conjunction with the main conference. Tutorials provide in-depth education on topics related to the Web.

Important Dates

  • Tutorial Proposal Submission: September 1, 2025
  • Notification: October 15, 2025

Submission Guidelines

Tutorial proposals should include the following information:

  • Tutorial title
  • Names and affiliations of presenters
  • Tutorial description and outline
  • Target audience and prerequisites
  • Proposed duration (half-day or full-day)
  • Previous presentations of the tutorial (if any)

Tutorial proposals should be submitted via email to tutorials@thewebconf2026.org.

PhD Symposium

The Web Conference 2026 invites PhD students to participate in the PhD Symposium, which provides an opportunity for doctoral students to present and discuss their research with senior researchers and other PhD students.

Important Dates

  • Submission Deadline: December 1, 2025
  • Notification: January 15, 2026

Submission Guidelines

PhD Symposium submissions should include:

  • A research summary (up to 4 pages) describing the problem, methodology, and results to date
  • A brief CV
  • A letter of recommendation from the PhD supervisor

Submissions should be made via the conference submission system (available in September 2025).
